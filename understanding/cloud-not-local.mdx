This is the thing that trips people up most often. Let's clear it up once and for all.

## The short version

**Your AI does not run on your computer.** It runs on a private server in the cloud — think of it like a dedicated computer that lives on the internet, that only you have access to.

This means it cannot see:
- Files on your desktop
- Your Downloads folder
- Photos on your phone
- Anything stored only on your device
- Your browser history or open tabs

## Why this matters

When people say _"connect to my folder"_ or _"access my files"_ — they often mean their local computer. That's not possible by default, because your AI is running somewhere else entirely.

It's the same reason you can't see your home computer's files from your phone unless you've specifically set something up (like cloud sync). The AI is in a completely different location.

## What you can do instead

**Upload files directly into the chat.** Click the attachment button in the chat window and upload any file. Your AI can then read it, summarize it, extract data, answer questions about it — whatever you need. This works for PDFs, Word docs, spreadsheets, images, and more.

**Connect cloud storage like Google Drive.** If your files are already in Google Drive, you can connect it and your AI can access them from there. [Connect Google Drive →](/connect/google-drive)

**Connect Gmail.** Your AI can read and respond to email once you connect it. [Connect Gmail →](/connect/gmail-google)

## A helpful way to think about it

Imagine you hired a very capable assistant who works from a separate office across town. They're great at their job, but they can only work with what you send them. You can fax them documents, email them files, give them access to your cloud storage — but they can't walk into your house and look through your filing cabinet.

Your AI is that assistant. It works from its own private office (the cloud), and it can only access what you give it access to.

## What "the cloud" actually means

"The cloud" just means computers that are connected to the internet and managed by someone else. When you use Gmail, Google Drive, iCloud, Dropbox, or Netflix — you're already using the cloud every day. Your AI is the same idea: it runs on a computer that's always on, always connected, and always available — you just access it through your browser.

The difference is that your AI's cloud computer is private — dedicated only to you, not shared with anyone else.
