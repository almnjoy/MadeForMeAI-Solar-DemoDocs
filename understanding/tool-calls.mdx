---
title: "It Paused — What's a Tool Call?"
description: Why your AI sometimes pauses mid-response and shows you what it's doing.
---

Sometimes when you ask your AI something, it doesn't just immediately reply. Instead, it pauses for a moment and shows you something like:

> _Searching the web…_
> _Reading file…_
> _Creating document…_

This is called a **tool call** — and it means your AI is actually doing something, not just talking.

## This is a good thing

When your AI uses a tool, it's taking action. It searched the web. It opened a file. It sent an email. It's not just generating text — it's doing real work.

Think of it like watching someone type at a keyboard. The pause is them working, not them being stuck.

## How long does it take?

Usually a few seconds. Complex tasks (like searching multiple sources, or working with a large file) might take 10–30 seconds. That's normal.

If it's been more than a minute and nothing has happened, try refreshing the page and asking again.

## What kinds of tools does it use?

- **Web search** — searching the internet for current information
- **File reading** — opening a document you uploaded or one in your connected storage
- **Email** — reading or sending email through your connected Gmail
- **File creation** — writing a document, spreadsheet, or note to your workspace
- **Calendar** — checking or adding events if you've connected Google Calendar

## It asked me to approve something — do I have to?

Sometimes your AI will ask for your confirmation before doing something significant — like sending an email or making a change. This is a safety feature, not a bug.

Just read what it's about to do, and if it looks right, tell it to go ahead: _"Yes, send it"_ or _"looks good, do it."_

If you don't want it to proceed, just say _"no"_ or _"stop"_ — or describe what you'd rather it do instead.

## It's not broken

The most common reaction people have when they first see a tool call is thinking something went wrong. It didn't. Your AI is working exactly as it should. Give it a moment.
