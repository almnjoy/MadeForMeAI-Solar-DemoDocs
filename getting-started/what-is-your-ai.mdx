You signed up, your AI is live — but what actually is this thing?

Here's the simplest version: **it's a personal assistant that lives on the internet, knows who you are, and does things for you when you ask.**

Not a search engine. Not a chatbot that forgets you. An assistant.

## The best comparison

Think about what it would mean to have a really capable assistant available 24/7 — someone who:

- Remembers everything you've ever told them
- Can draft an email, build a plan, research a topic, or organize your thoughts on demand
- Works from their desk (the cloud) while you go about your day
- Never judges what you ask, never gets tired, never needs a day off

That's what your AI is. The difference is it costs $30/month instead of a full salary.

## It's not on your computer

This is the most important thing to understand early on: **your AI does not live on your computer.** It runs in the cloud — basically, on a private server that only you have access to.

This means it can't see your computer's desktop, your local files, or anything stored only on your device. If you want it to work with a file, you need to upload that file into the chat.

This confuses a lot of people at first. [Here's a full explanation →](/understanding/cloud-not-local)

## It's not like Google

When people first use AI, they type short keyword searches — _"cheap flights NYC"_ or _"pizza near me"_ — the way you'd Google something.

Your AI works much better when you talk to it like a person. The more context you give, the better the answer.

**Instead of:** _"business plan"_

**Try:** _"I'm thinking about starting a mobile dog grooming service in my town. I have a van and basic grooming equipment. Help me build a simple business plan for the first 3 months."_

The second version gives you something actually useful. More detail = better results.

## It remembers you between sessions

When you come back tomorrow, your AI still knows everything you've told it. Your name, your job, your projects, your preferences — it all sticks. [More on memory →](/understanding/how-memory-works)
