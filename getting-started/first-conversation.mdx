Your AI is ready. Now what?

The best thing you can do in your first conversation is **introduce yourself and give it some context about your life.** The more it knows about you upfront, the more useful every future conversation will be.

## Start here: introduce yourself

Copy this, fill it in, and send it:

```
My name is [your name]. I [what you do for work or at home]. I'm using this AI to help me with [1-2 things you want to use it for]. Some things about me that might be useful: [anything relevant — business type, family situation, location, goals, etc.]
```

For example:

> _My name is Maria. I run a small event planning business out of Austin, Texas. I'm using this AI to help me write proposals, manage my schedule, and brainstorm ideas for clients. I'm not very technical and I prefer simple, direct answers._

Your AI will remember all of that forever. You never have to repeat it.

## Things to try in your first week

**Get organized:**
- _"Help me make a to-do list for this week."_
- _"I have a meeting tomorrow about [topic]. Help me prepare."_
- _"Here's what I'm working on right now: [describe it]. What should I do first?"_

**Write something:**
- _"Write a professional follow-up email to a client who hasn't responded in two weeks."_
- _"Help me write a bio for my Instagram page. I'm a [what you do]."_
- _"Draft a message to my team about [topic]."_

**Research something:**
- _"Search the web and give me a quick summary of [topic]."_
- _"What are the best ways for a small business to get more local customers?"_
- _"I'm thinking about [decision]. What should I consider?"_

**Make a plan:**
- _"I want to launch [something] in 60 days. Build me a week-by-week plan."_
- _"I have [X amount] to spend on marketing this month. How should I use it?"_

## Tips that make a big difference

**Give context, not just keywords.** The more detail you give, the better the result. Don't Google it — explain it.

**Just talk to it.** You don't need special commands or "prompts." Plain sentences work great. _"Write me an email"_ is fine. _"Can you help me with something?"_ is fine. Just talk.

**If the first answer isn't quite right, say so.** _"That's a bit too formal — make it more casual."_ _"Shorter please."_ _"Add more detail about the pricing."_ Your AI responds to feedback exactly like a person would.

**Tell it things you want it to remember.** _"Remember that I prefer bullet points over long paragraphs."_ _"My business partner's name is Jamie."_ It saves this automatically.

## The most common mistake

People open it and type something really vague like _"help me with my business"_ — and then feel like the AI didn't do much.

Give it something specific to work with. The vaguer the ask, the vaguer the answer. That's true for any assistant, human or AI.
