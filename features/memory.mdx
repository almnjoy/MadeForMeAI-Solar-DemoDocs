MadeForMeAI remembers what you tell it — and carries that knowledge into every future conversation. Unlike a standard AI chat that forgets everything the moment you close the window, your AI builds a persistent picture of you over time. The more you use it, the better it knows you.

## What your AI remembers

Your AI stores everything you share with it, across all of your conversations:

- **Facts about you** — your job, your goals, your projects
- **Your preferences** — how you like tasks done, the tone you prefer in writing
- **Ongoing work** — projects in progress, decisions you've made, deadlines you're tracking
- **People and places** — clients, colleagues, and locations you've mentioned

## How memory works in practice

You don't have to do anything special to activate memory. Just talk to your AI the way you normally would — it handles the rest.

    > "I'm trying to launch my online store by June 1st. I'm behind on the product photos."

    **Thursday:**
    > "How am I doing on that June deadline?"

    > "I hate long emails. Keep everything short and direct."

## Telling your AI to remember something specific

If you want to make sure your AI holds on to a particular detail, just say so naturally:

> *"Remember that my client Sarah prefers to be contacted on Tuesdays."*

> *"Keep in mind that I'm vegetarian when helping me plan meals."*

Your AI will treat those as explicit notes and carry them forward.

## Telling your AI to forget something

If something is no longer relevant, you can ask your AI to remove it from memory just as easily:

> *"Forget what I told you about the Johnson project — that's been cancelled."*

Your AI will stop referencing that information going forward.

## Is my memory private?

Yes. Your memory is stored only in your private instance. Nobody else can read it, access it, or use it to train any AI model — not even MadeForMeAI's own systems.
