Your AI isn't limited to what it already knows. When you ask a question that requires up-to-date information, it searches the web and brings back current results — news, prices, research, comparisons, and more. You don't have to think about when to trigger a search; your AI figures that out on its own.

## How to use web search

Just ask your question naturally. If your AI determines that a live search would give you a better answer, it searches automatically.

Some examples of questions that will trigger a search:

- *"What's the weather in Chicago this weekend?"*
- *"Find me the latest news about [topic]."*
- *"What's the current price of [product]?"*
- *"Research competitors for a meal prep delivery service."*

You don't need to say "search the web" — your AI decides when a search is the right move.

## Limitations

Web search pulls from the publicly available web. Keep the following in mind:

- Your AI cannot access pages behind logins, paywalls, or private networks.
- Results depend on what's publicly available — if information isn't online, your AI won't find it.
- For very recent events (within the last few hours), results may be limited or incomplete.
