---
title: "Upload, Analyze, and Work with Files in MadeForMeAI"
description: "Send PDFs, spreadsheets, images, and code files to your MadeForMeAI assistant and ask it to summarize, analyze, rewrite, or extract information."
---

You can hand your AI a file and ask it to do real work with it — summarize a contract, pull numbers from a spreadsheet, describe what's in a screenshot, or fix errors in a document. There's no need to copy and paste content into the chat; just upload the file and tell your AI what you need.

## Supported file types

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Documents">
    - PDF
    - Word (.docx)
    - Plain text files
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Spreadsheets">
    - CSV
    - Excel
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Images">
    - PNG
    - JPG
    - Screenshots
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Code">
    Any plain text file (`.js`, `.py`, `.ts`, `.html`, etc.)
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## How to upload a file

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the chat input">
    Navigate to your AI's chat interface as you normally would.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the attachment icon">
    Look for the attachment icon in the chat input area and click it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select your file">
    Choose the file you want to upload from your device.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your message and send">
    Type what you want your AI to do with the file, then send the message.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to ask your AI to do

Once your file is uploaded, describe what you need in plain language:

- *"Summarize this contract in plain English."*
- *"What are the key numbers in this spreadsheet?"*
- *"Find any errors in this document."*
- *"Rewrite this in a friendlier tone."*
- *"What's in this screenshot?"*

## Tips for better results

<Tip>
  You can upload multiple files in a single message — your AI will work with all of them at once.
</Tip>

- **Larger files take a moment to process.** Give it a few seconds before assuming something went wrong.
- **Be specific with long documents.** Instead of "read this," try "Focus on section 3" or "Summarize the key terms in pages 4–6." The more targeted your request, the more useful the response.
