The Job Search Agent is a dedicated AI that runs in the background, searching job boards on a schedule, scoring every posting against your resume and preferences, and notifying you only when something actually matches. You set it up once — it keeps running.

It works for **any job type**: retail, healthcare, tech, trades, remote, local, entry-level, senior — doesn't matter. You tell it what you're looking for and it figures out where to look.

---

## How to get it

Open your AI and say one of these:

- _"Set up a job search agent"_
- _"Find me jobs"_
- _"I want help finding work"_
- _"Set up job hunting for me"_

Your AI will hand off to the Setup Assistant, which walks you through the full onboarding. Takes about 5 minutes.

---

## What onboarding covers

The Setup Assistant collects everything it needs through a friendly conversation — not a form. Here's what it asks:

**What you're looking for**
- Job titles, keywords, and industry
- Location, remote preference, or fully remote only
- Any companies or industries to avoid

**How to score jobs**
- Green flags — things that make a job a strong fit (technologies, work style, pay range, etc.)
- Yellow flags — not dealbreakers but not ideal
- Red flags — things that would make you skip a posting entirely
- Your notification threshold (default: 7 out of 10)

**Your resume**
- Upload one or more resume files (`.docx` or `.pdf`)
- For each one, you tell it what type of role it's best for — the agent picks the right resume for each match automatically

**How often to run**
- Every 4 hours, twice daily, daily, or weekly — your call

**Where to send results**
- In this chat (always works)
- Discord, Telegram, or WhatsApp if you've connected them

**Google Sheets tracking (optional)**
- The agent can track every job it finds in a Google Sheet — title, company, link, score, reasoning, status
- Requires a free Google service account (the Setup Assistant walks you through it — about 5 minutes)
- If you skip this, results come straight to chat

---

## How the scoring works

Every job posting gets a score from 1 to 10 based on your profile:

- **Green flag matches** push the score up
- **Yellow flag matches** pull it down slightly
- **Red flag matches** pull it down hard
- **Compensation mentioned** adds a small bonus

Only jobs that hit your threshold (default: 7/10) trigger a notification. Everything else is logged silently.

For each strong match, the agent also:
- Writes a brief cover note based on why it scored well
- Picks the right resume version if you uploaded more than one

---

## How the job search works

The agent searches the web before every run and writes the results to a local file. It handles any job type — it figures out which job boards and search queries make sense for your role and location. RSS feeds are used where available; web search fills in the rest.

Every job gets a stable ID so duplicates are skipped automatically across runs. You won't see the same posting twice.

## Running it manually

After setup, just say:

- _"Run jobs now"_
- _"Check for jobs"_
- _"Any new matches?"_

The agent will do a fresh search and report back immediately.

## Updating your preferences

Say:

- _"Update my job profile"_
- _"Change my job search preferences"_
- _"Add 'remote only' to my search"_

The agent reads your current profile, applies the change, re-searches, and confirms.

---

## Uploading a new resume

Just upload the file to your AI and say:
_"Add this to my job search resumes"_ or _"Use this resume for tech roles."_

The agent saves it and updates your profile.

## Google Sheets setup (optional but recommended)

If you want a running log of every job the agent finds — not just the ones that notify you — the agent can write everything to a Google Sheet automatically.

Each row includes: job ID, title, company, URL, source, date posted, score, reasoning, cover note, status, and date added.

The Setup Assistant walks you through the one-time setup:
1. Create a free Google Cloud project
2. Enable the Sheets API
3. Create a service account and download the JSON key
4. Share your target Google Sheet with the service account email
5. Give the agent your Sheet ID and tab name

After that, every run populates the sheet automatically. You can review, filter, and track applications directly in Google Sheets.

<Note>Google creates new sheets with a tab named **Sheet1** by default. When the Setup Assistant asks for your tab name, confirm whether you've renamed it. If you haven't, just say "Sheet1."</Note>
