Once connected, your AI lives in your Discord server as a private bot. You can message it in any channel you invite it to, and it responds just like any other AI conversation.

## Before you start

You need:
- A Discord account with access to the server you want to add the bot to
- Server admin rights, or a server admin who can approve bot invites
- Access to your AI's chat window (open `yourname.madeformeai.com` in a browser)

---

## How to set it up

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ask your Setup Assistant">
    Open your AI's chat and type:

    _"Set up Discord for me"_

    The Setup Assistant will guide you through every step one at a time.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a Discord application">
    Your Setup Assistant will walk you to the **Discord Developer Portal** at [discord.com/developers/applications](https://discord.com/developers/applications).

    Click **New Application**, give it a name (something like `My AI Assistant`), and click **Create**. This is the container for your bot.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a bot and copy the token">
    Inside your new application, open **Bot** in the left sidebar. Click **Add Bot** if prompted.

    Under **Token**, click **Reset Token** (or **View Token**) and copy it. This is what connects your AI to Discord.

    <Warning>Treat this token like a password. Don't post it in public Discord channels or share it anywhere. If it ever gets exposed, regenerate it immediately in the Developer Portal.</Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable the required intents">
    Still on the **Bot** page, scroll down to **Privileged Gateway Intents** and turn on:

    - **Message Content Intent** — lets your bot read messages in channels
    - **Server Members Intent** — lets your bot look up members and roles

    Save your changes.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Invite the bot to your server">
    Go to **OAuth2 → URL Generator** in the left sidebar.

    Under **Scopes**, select `bot` and `applications.commands`.

    Under **Bot Permissions**, select at minimum:
    - View Channels
    - Send Messages
    - Read Message History
    - Send Messages in Threads
    - Add Reactions
    - Attach Files
    - Embed Links
    - Use Slash Commands

    Copy the generated URL, open it in your browser, and choose your server. Click **Authorize**.

    If Discord says you don't have permission to add apps, you'll need a server admin to do this step.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Give the token to your Setup Assistant">
    Paste the bot token back into your Setup Assistant's chat. It will handle all the configuration, restart what it needs to, and confirm when Discord is connected.

    Once done, mention the bot in a Discord channel to test it.

    <Note>If you pasted the token in chat during setup, rotate it afterward in the Discord Developer Portal → Bot → Reset Token. This is a good security habit.</Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Using your AI on Discord

Once connected, just mention the bot in any channel it has access to:

- _"@MyAI what's the weather in Chicago today?"_
- _"@MyAI draft a message to the team about Friday's meeting"_
- _"@MyAI search the web for the latest on [topic]"_

The bot can also respond in threads and DMs depending on how you've set up permissions.

---

## Adding more channels

By default your bot can respond anywhere it has permissions in your server. If you want to restrict or expand which specific channels it listens in, tell your Setup Assistant:

_"Add the #general channel to my Discord bot"_

It will walk you through getting the channel ID from Discord (Developer Mode → right-click channel → Copy Channel ID) and handle the rest.

---

## Disconnect Discord

Tell your Setup Assistant: _"Disconnect Discord"_ and it will remove the connection.

---

## Troubleshooting

**Bot is online but doesn't respond to messages**
Make sure **Message Content Intent** is enabled in the Developer Portal. Without it, the bot sees messages arrive but can't read the text.

**"Missing Permissions" errors**
Check that the bot role has the right permissions in your server, and that no channel-specific overrides are blocking it.

**Token rejected / unauthorized error**
Regenerate the token in the Developer Portal and run setup again.
